🔄 The Four Pipeline Segments

Floating-point numbers X = A × 2^a and Y = B × 2^b are processed through four dedicated segments:

1
Compare Exponents

Calculate the difference between exponents (a - b) through subtraction

2
Align Mantissas

Shift mantissa of smaller exponent number to match the larger. Choose result exponent

3
Add/Subtract

Perform arithmetic operation on the aligned mantissas

4
Normalize Result

Shift mantissa and adjust exponent to normalized floating-point form

Segment Operation Function
Segment 1 Compare Exponents Determines difference between exponents through subtraction
Segment 2 Align Mantissas Adjusts mantissa to match larger exponent via shifting
Segment 3 Add/Subtract Mantissas Performs arithmetic operation on aligned mantissas
Segment 4 Normalize Results Shifts mantissa and adjusts exponent for normalized form

📊 Numerical Illustration

Example: Adding Two Floating-Point Numbers

Given: X = 0.9504 × 10³ and Y = 0.8200 × 10²

Step 1: Compare Exponents
Difference: 3 - 2 = 1
Step 2: Align Mantissas
X is larger, so shift Y's mantissa right by 1 position:
X = 0.9504 × 10³
Y = 0.0820 × 10³
Step 3: Add Mantissas
Z = (0.9504 + 0.0820) × 10³ = 1.0324 × 10³
Step 4: Normalize Result
Mantissa > 1, shift left and increment exponent:
Z = 0.10324 × 10⁴

💡 Key Benefits

Overlapped Execution: Different operands can be processed simultaneously in different pipeline stages, dramatically increasing throughput.

Efficient Resource Usage: Each segment specializes in one operation, optimizing hardware utilization.

High Performance: Essential for scientific computing, graphics processing, and machine learning applications.
