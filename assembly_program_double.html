💡 Why Double-Precision?
Early computers had limited word sizes (8 or 16 bits). To handle larger numbers, programmers used "double-precision" arithmetic - splitting large numbers into multiple smaller parts and processing them with carry propagation. This technique is still used today in cryptography and big integer arithmetic!

🔍 Understanding Binary Addition
Each bit position adds independently, just like decimal addition. When the sum of two bits exceeds 1, we generate a carry to the next position. In double-precision arithmetic, the carry from the low word must be added to the high word calculation.

How it works:
  1. Enter two numbers in hexadecimal (0x format) or decimal
  2. Numbers are automatically split into high and low 16-bit words
  3. Low words are added first, generating a carry if needed
  4. High words are added with the carry from the low word addition
  5. Final result combines both high and low results
