🎯 What is Decimal Division?

Decimal division is similar to binary division, but operates in base-10 (digits 0-9). The process involves repeated subtraction and shifting, where you subtract the divisor as many times as possible until a negative remainder results.

💡 Key Concept: The remainder is restored by adding back the divisor when subtraction results in a negative value. The digit representing the number of subtractions excludes the one that caused the negative remainder.

📋 The Algorithm

Step-by-Step Process:

1 Initialize: Place dividend in register AQ, divisor in register B. Set SC (step counter) to k.
2 Shift Left: Shift AQ left with most significant digit placed in Ae.
3 Subtract: Perform A ← A - B (subtract divisor from partial remainder).
4 Check Sign: If A < B (negative result), restore by A ← A + B and set QL = 0. Otherwise, set QL ← QL + 1.
5 Decrement: Decrease SC by 1. If SC ≠ 0, repeat from step 2.
6 Complete: Quotient is in Q, remainder is in A.

📝 Example Walkthrough

Example: Divide decimal numbers

Process:

  1. The partial remainder and quotient bits are shifted left
  2. The process repeats k times to form k quotient digits
  3. The remainder is found in register A
  4. Quotient digits are accumulated in register Q
⚠️ Important: When the result is negative (A < B), the divisor is added back to restore the partial remainder, and QL stays at 0. The quotient digit equals the number of times divisor was subtracted from the dividend.
