⚡ Floating-Point Arithmetic Operations

Computer Arithmetic

📘 Overview

It explores the hardware implementation of arithmetic operations for floating-point binary numbers. These operations are more complex and slower than fixed-point computations, often requiring specialized subroutines when dedicated hardware isn't available.

🔢 Floating-Point Structure

Mantissa

A signed, fixed-point number representing the significant digits of the value.

Exponent

Designates the position of the decimal (or binary) point in the number.

Normalization

Ensures maximum precision by making the most significant digit nonzero.

m × re
where m = mantissa, r = radix (2 for binary), e = exponent

🎯 Arithmetic Operations

+

Addition & Subtraction

The most complex floating-point operations requiring exponent alignment:

  • Compare the exponents of both operands
  • Shift the mantissa of the smaller number (denormalization)
  • Align exponents until they are equal
  • Perform fixed-point addition/subtraction on mantissas
  • Normalize the result by adjusting mantissa and exponent
×

Multiplication

Simpler alignment requirements than addition:

  • Add the exponents together
  • Multiply the mantissas
  • Determine the sign of the result
  • Normalize the final product
÷

Division

Similar to multiplication with reciprocal operations:

  • Subtract the divisor's exponent from dividend's
  • Divide the mantissas
  • Determine the sign of the quotient
  • Normalize the final quotient
